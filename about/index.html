About Dashcam

Built after a crash made “no proof” feel unacceptable.

Dashcam started from a simple frustration: after a crash, there was no clean way to prove what happened. Buying another camera felt like more hardware instead of a better answer.

Back to home Open support guide

The idea

Use the phone already in the car. Keep the proof local.

The point of Dashcam is not to imitate a dedicated camera. The point is to give an iPhone a calmer, more credible dashcam mode that keeps route guidance, recording, and incident protection together.

That is why the product is careful about what it claims. If capture stops, if heat matters, or if a setup pushes past what is realistic, the product should say so.

Local-first

No company clip cloud required

Core processing happens on device. Optional iCloud upload stays tied to the user’s Apple account.

Platform honesty

Foreground-only means foreground-only

If another app takes over the screen, capture stops. Dashcam treats that as a product truth, not a fine-print surprise.

Evidence continuity

Protect the clip that matters

The goal is preserving the incident window with context intact, not saving endless footage nobody can review.

Read the details

Support, privacy, and product limits should be easy to inspect.

The best version of this site reads like the shipped app: clear about storage, clear about limits, and calm about what the product is for.
