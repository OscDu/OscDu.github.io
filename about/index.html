About Dashcam

Built because no proof is not good enough.

Dashcam started from a simple frustration: after a crash, there was no clean way to show what happened. The better answer was not another device to remember. It was a more useful mode for the phone already in the car.

Product principle

If recording stops, if heat matters, or if a setup is not realistic, the app should say so clearly before trust breaks.

The idea

Use the phone already in the car. Keep the proof under your control.

The point is not to imitate every dedicated camera. The point is to make the iPhone credible for everyday evidence: road recording, route context, clear warnings, and deliberate export in one place.

01

Map-first recording

Drive Mode keeps navigation context and recording state visible so the app is useful during the actual drive, not just after it.

02

Honest platform limits

Foreground-only means foreground-only. If another app takes over, Dashcam treats that as product truth, not fine print.

03

Local-first evidence

Core clips stay in the local library unless you export them or choose optional iCloud upload with your Apple account.

Read the details

Support, privacy, and limits should be easy to inspect.

The site should read like the app: calm about what it does, clear about storage, and direct about where iOS draws the line.
