Arbitration and Class Action Waiver (v1)

Last updated: 2026-02-15 Effective date: 2026-02-15

This Arbitration and Class Action Waiver section is incorporated into the Dashcam App Terms of Use (terms.v1.md).

1. Agreement to Arbitrate

To the maximum extent permitted by law, you and MagSafe Dashcam LLC agree to resolve any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to the App or the Terms through final and binding arbitration, rather than in court, except as expressly provided below.

This agreement is governed by the U.S. Federal Arbitration Act (FAA), 9 U.S.C. Sections 1-16.

2. Governing Rules and Administrator

Arbitration will be administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) under its Consumer Arbitration Rules, as modified by this section.

3. Informal Resolution First

Before filing arbitration, either party must provide written notice of the dispute and a 30-day period to attempt informal resolution.

Notice from you must include:

  • your full name;
  • the email associated with your App Store account (if available);
  • a description of the dispute and requested relief;
  • your signature (physical or electronic).

Send notice to: legal@magsafedashcam.com.

4. Opt-Out Right

You may opt out of this arbitration agreement within 30 days of first accepting the Terms by sending a written opt-out notice to legal@magsafedashcam.com with:

  • full name;
  • email associated with your App usage, if any;
  • clear statement that you opt out of arbitration;
  • date and signature.

If you opt out, all other parts of the Terms remain in effect.

5. Small Claims Carve-Out

Either party may bring an individual claim in small claims court instead of arbitration if the claim qualifies and remains in that court on an individual basis.

6. Injunctive Relief Carve-Out

Either party may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief in court when reasonably necessary to protect intellectual property, confidentiality, or security pending completion of arbitration.

7. Class and Representative Action Waiver

To the maximum extent permitted by law, disputes must be brought only on an individual basis. Neither party may participate as a plaintiff or class member in any class, collective, consolidated, coordinated, or representative proceeding.

The arbitrator may award relief only to the individual party seeking relief and only to the extent necessary to resolve that party's individual claim.

8. Procedure and Location

Unless the parties agree otherwise, arbitration may proceed by documents, remote hearing, or in-person hearing as determined by the arbitrator under AAA rules.

The legal seat and place of arbitration will be in California, unless applicable law requires another forum.

9. Fees

Fees will be allocated as required by AAA rules and applicable law. Each side bears its own attorneys' fees except where a statute, rule, or arbitrator decision provides otherwise.

10. Governing Law

The Terms and this arbitration agreement are governed by California law (without regard to conflict-of-law rules), except where preempted by federal law, including the FAA.

11. Severability

If any part of this section is found unenforceable, the remainder remains enforceable, except that if the class-action waiver is unenforceable as to a particular claim and that determination is not subject to appeal, that claim will proceed in a court of competent jurisdiction and not in arbitration.
